7. Key Findings & Recommendations

CRITICAL: 1 lead from $10,195 spend. The conversion tracking or landing page funnel is severely broken. 4,060 clicks at 5.18% CTR means the ads are working - people ARE clicking. The breakdown is happening post-click. Either: (a) the pixel/event isn't firing, (b) the landing page isn't converting, or (c) the "Submit Application" event isn't being tracked properly.
The ads themselves are performing well. 5.18% CTR at $2.51 CPC for a cold LEADS campaign is solid. The creative copy is engaging people. The problem is downstream, not in the ads.
Hook patterns that dominate: "If you're a practitioner..." 6 out of 23 creatives open with identity-based hooks. This is the most common pattern and it's working (5.18% overall CTR). The sarcasm/mockery hooks (alternating caps) are the second most common and create pattern interrupts.
"Install Patient Acquisition That Runs Itself" is the most-used headline appearing in 5 creatives. It's the clearest benefit statement. However, headline diversity is actually quite good across the set - no single headline dominates too heavily.
All creatives are static images. No video. For a complex SaaS offer targeting practitioners, video would likely outperform. The Video Views campaign is running separately ($2,200 spend, 190K impressions) but isn't connected to the Sub App conversion funnel.
Frequency is too high on Ad Set 1 (3.07x). Audience saturation is a real risk. 76.9% of budget going to one ad set that's already at 3x frequency. Rotate budget to Ad Set 3 which has the best efficiency metrics and lowest frequency (1.34x).
Common copy DNA across all 23 creatives:
- All reference "7 AI employees" or "specialized AI agents"
- All mention "$41M in results" or "250+ calls with practitioners"
- All position against agencies ($5K-$10K/month retainers)
- All use "sickcare" as the enemy frame
- All end with "JonCameron" signature
- All link to olympus.etho.net
- All use LEARN_MORE CTA
